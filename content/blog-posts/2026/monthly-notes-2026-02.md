This month was a story in two parts. I had a great time with Warhammer and a not-so-great time with everything else.

## Warhammer

As I mentioned [last month](/blog/monthly-notes-january-2026), I’ve signed up for a tournament in March and need to paint a _lot_ of models. After painting 21 models last month, I managed to paint 46 [models](/tags/nighthaunt/) this month. Which is crazy to me, as it’s more than I’ve painted for all my other armies combined.

It’s a mixed bag. I’m pleased with the quantity and quality of my work – if anything, the quality of it improved as time went on – but it’s been a slog. It is so far above my normal level of painting that I am completely burnt out, and I am looking forward to being done next week – only three models left – and being able to focus on my other interests again.

Seeing the whole army assembled and painted is a fantastic feeling, especially with this quantity of models; it looks sick. After a break, I hope to find the motivation to continue painting this year—albeit at a more relaxed pace—as I have many, many models to paint.

### First game of AoS

I got to play my first game of [Age of Sigmar](https://www.warhammer.com/en-GB/explore/age-of-sigmar) this month, and it’s good.

It’s both weirdly similar and different to [40k](https://www.warhammer.com/en-GB/explore/warhammer-40000). The system felt familiar, and I made a few mistakes with the few false friends that exist, but overall, it was much less difficult than I feared. It’s a much more relaxed game – at least it felt less sweaty and precise than 40k. The lack of terrain is also a nice bonus, both visually and for setup.

I will be playing more of this. I love [my 40k armies](/tags/warhammer-40k/) and I _really_ want to finish them, but I can't deny that my interest in Warhammer [started with AoS](/tags/age-of-sigmar/) and it continues to capture my attention, and now knowing how it plays has piqued my interest again. Though I’m sure the new edition of 40k will make me swing the other way again.

## Health

This month was supposed to be the start of my monthly training cycles, and the first two weeks were promising: I felt stronger, my results showed a return to form, and the excitement of improvement was building.

### The incident

Then I was hit by a car. I’m fine, the bike and I got off lightly and I walked away just bruised and scraped (I did go get checked out properly). Thankfully, it was at a very low speed. 

This was my first time really coming off the bike. I did have a smaller incident earlier this year where I lost a fight with a patch of ice, and I am thankful that it was not overly dramatic; it could’ve been so much worse. 

As required, I reported it to the police, and, to no one's surprise, they deemed that nothing criminal had occurred. I know, absolute shocker. Entering a roundabout without looking and hitting a cyclist and nearly hitting the car behind her is completely normal and fine behaviour. Whatever.

In retrospect, I’m not sure how I feel about this. I think I've been downplaying it, but my only focus was on getting back on the bike and not letting any doubts creep in. And while I am more conscious of cars than before, I don’t think there's been a significant knock to my confidence. Though I have changed my commute route to avoid that roundabout, I always joked that it was sketchy.

### Parkrun

Unsurprisingly, I didn’t run that week. Instead, I volunteered to marshal at my regular [parkrun](https://www.parkrun.org.uk/). It was weird being on the other side. I hadn’t realised how many people attended, or how fast some were. Overall, it was rewarding. I want to do it again, but I don’t know how to balance it with my running schedule.

The following week, though, I felt good enough to run, and it was good to be back on it. It was a little uncomfortable where the skin was tight, but mechanically, everything felt good, which was a huge relief.
